Mental health signs

  1. consistent energy levels
  2. feeling connected to one's surroundings and community
  3. and having a sense of meaning and purpose in life.

It's important to prioritize self-care habits like exercise, meditation, and healthy eating to promote mental well-being.

Self-evaluation

  • Knowing your strengths and weaknesses is an important step in maintaining mental health.
  • Strengths can be utilized to enhance confidence and achieve goals while weaknesses can be addressed through targeted growth practices.
  • A self-assessment can help identify areas of improvement and set intentions to work on those weaknesses.

Anger management

  • Anger is a normal and natural emotion but can become a problem when it leads to unhealthy and destructive behavior.
  • Managing anger involves recognizing triggers, expressing feelings constructively, and practicing self-control techniques like deep breathing or mindfulness.

Burnout syndrome

  • Burnout is a state of exhaustion, both physical and emotional, that can occur in response to prolonged stress and lack of work-life balance. It can manifest itself through symptoms like fatigue, lack of motivation, and a sense of detachment from work. Self-care, boundaries, and self-reflection are important for preventing and managing burnout.

Academic stress & test anxiety

  • Academic stress and test anxiety can negatively impact mental health by causing feelings of overwhelm, insecurity, and low self-confidence. Self-compassion and effective study strategies can help manage academic stress and test anxiety such as time management, regular breaks, and exercise.

Problem solving behaviour

  • Effective problem-solving skills can foster resilience and confidence in tackling challenges. When approaching conflicts, it's important to focus on identifying the problem, exploring options, and choosing a course of action. Mindfulness and self-compassion can help manage emotional reactions during the problem-solving process.

Mental health & technology

Digital detox

  • Our dependence on technology can have negative effects on mental health through increased stress, anxiety, and isolation. Taking a break from digital devices through a digital detox can restore balance and promote mindfulness and resilience.

Conflict & resolution

  • Healthy conflict includes active listening, clear communication, and finding common ground. Conflict resolution skills, including active listening, empathetic communication, and seeking compromise, can enhance relationships and promote greater mental well-being.

Decision making

  • Effective decision making skills require self-reflection, seeking information and multiple perspectives, and considering potential outcomes. When making important decisions, it's important to be aware of one's cognitive biases and to practice critical thinking.

Personal disorganization

  • Disorganization can lead to stress and negative self-talk. Practicing effective storage solutions, such as decluttering and organizing routines, can promote mental well-being by reducing stress and improving confidence.

Mindfulness

  • Mindfulness involves focusing on the present moment, without judgment, and is a powerful tool for mental wellness. Incorporating mindfulness practices into daily life, such as meditation or breathing exercises, can improve emotional regulation and promote resilience.

Social support

  • Social support is essential for promoting mental health and well-being. Having individuals to confide in, to express emotions, and to provide emotional and practical help can improve self-esteem and reduce stress.

Recreation & stress relief activity

  • Recreation and stress relief activities help to replenish energy, reduce stress, and promote mental well-being. Engaging in activities that bring joy and relaxation, such as exercise, yoga, or spending time in nature, can be beneficial for overall health.
